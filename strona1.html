1. Royal Bathrooms

It is one of the most beautiful garden not only in Poland, but also in Europe. Warsaw residents owe this magical place to Stanisław Augustus, who in the 17th century acquired a suburban animal from Stanisław Lubomirski and then turned it into a summer residence.

2.Palace of Culture and Science

It is really a showcase of Warsaw and the tallest building in Poland (it is 237 m high). The Palace of Culture and Science is the work of social realism. The building, which was built between 1952 and 1955, was a kind of "gift" of the Soviet Union for Poland.

3.Copernicus Science Center

Copernicus Science Center is an unusual place on the map of tourist attractions of Warsaw. It is a peculiar museum, opened in 2010, which combines the features of a traditional exhibition and scientific laboratory. There are 6 interactive permanent exhibitions, temporary exhibitions, educational laboratories and an unusual planetarium "Copernicus Sky", on which the screen, in addition to the stars, you can watch educational and popular science films.
